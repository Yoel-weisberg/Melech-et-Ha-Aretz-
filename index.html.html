1. "A Taste of Ancient Israel" Culinary Workshop

$850 up to 7 guests

A boutique culinary experience inspired by the agricultural traditions, flavors, and rich hospitality of the Land of Israel. Together, families prepare traditional foods rooted in the ancient culinary culture of the region using fresh herbs, olive oil, stone-ground spices, open fire, and handmade breads.

What Your Family Prepares Together:

Homemade hummus & fresh tehina
Rich homemade Labaneh
Traditional pan-fired Shakshuka
Fresh seasonal salad with wild herbs
Fresh laffa bread baked on a traditional saj
Spiced Turkish coffee & herbal wild tea

Families grind heritage spices, knead and bake bread over the flames, and discover the thousands-of-years history of ancient oil, grains, and communal dining. Concludes with a beautiful feast served in nature or inside an atmospheric private Jerusalem home.

Meet Hallel Weisberg

Nachlaot Native • Certified Guide • Artisan

Hallel Weisberg is a Jerusalem-based educator, guide, and craftswoman, born and raised in the historic, winding alleyways of Nachlaot.

As a certified guide, Hallel leads immersive hands-on workshops for families and groups rooted in ancient crafts and meaningful creation. Her workshops draw heavily from her deep-seated love of nature, ancient skills, and simple human connection.

Through her years of experience working with children, adults, and vibrant multi-generational family units, Hallel creates uniquely warm, welcoming settings where visitors can intentionally slow down, explore together, and connect with Jewish traditions beyond conventional touring.

"My passion is inviting families to pause the busy itinerary, step into the slow presence of Jerusalem's history, and create physical souvenirs using materials that have built our history for thousands of years."
